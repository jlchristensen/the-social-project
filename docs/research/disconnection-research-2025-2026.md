# Research Report: Human Disconnection — 2025-2026 Studies

**Researched:** April 10, 2026
**Purpose:** Update the "Why This Matters" homepage stats with the most recent, verifiable data on loneliness, social disconnection, and screen time.

---

## Why This Matters

The Social Project's homepage stats were sourced from 2020-2023 data. While those numbers are still accurate, fresher research paints an even more urgent picture — and using current data builds credibility with an audience that can spot stale stats.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Gen Z Is the Loneliest Generation on Record

- **67% of Gen Z classify as lonely** on the UCLA Loneliness Scale
- Millennials: 65%, Gen X: 60%, Boomers: 44%
- Gen Z is **2× lonelier than Baby Boomers**
- Source: Cigna 2025 Loneliness in America Report (based on 2024 Vitality in America study, 7,500 U.S. adults surveyed)
- Link: https://newsroom.thecignagroup.com/loneliness-in-america
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — major health insurer's annual longitudinal survey

### 2. Americans Socialize Just 34 Minutes Per Day

- In 2024, Americans spent an average of **0.56 hours (≈34 minutes) per day socializing and communicating** during leisure time
- For context: in 2003, young adults (15-24) spent ~150 minutes/day with friends in person
- That's a decline from 2.5 hours to about half an hour — across two decades
- Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, American Time Use Survey 2024 (released June 2025)
- Link: https://www.bls.gov/news.release/atus.t11a.htm
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — U.S. government official dataset

### 3. Teens Spend 5 Hours/Day on Social Media

- The average U.S. teen now spends **nearly 5 hours per day on social media**
- 25% of 13-14-year-olds spend **7+ hours daily** on social media
- Social media adoption in the early 2010s contributed to population-level increases in mental illness by the mid-2010s
- Source: World Happiness Report 2026, Chapter: "Social media is harming adolescents at a scale large enough to cause changes at the population level"
- Link: https://worldhappiness.report/ed/2026/social-media-is-harming-adolescents-at-a-scale-large-enough-to-cause-changes-at-the-population-level/
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — peer-reviewed chapter in annual global research report

### 4. 1 in 4 People Worldwide Feel Lonely (1+ Billion)

- **24% of the global population** reported feeling "very lonely" or "fairly lonely"
- That represents more than **1 billion people** worldwide
- Young adults (19-29) report the highest rates globally at 27%
- Lonely people are 36 percentage points more likely to experience sadness
- Source: Meta-Gallup State of Social Connections study (142 countries, ~1,000 participants per country)
- Link: https://news.gallup.com/opinion/gallup/512618/almost-quarter-world-feels-lonely.aspx
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — Gallup global poll, gold standard for international survey data

### 5. Teen Girls' Mental Health Has Collapsed

- **57% of teen girls** report persistent sadness or hopelessness (up from 36% in 2011)
- **30% have seriously considered suicide** (up from 19% in 2011)
- This decline began around 2012 (pre-COVID) and coincides with smartphone/social media adoption
- Source: CDC Youth Risk Behavior Survey, cited in World Happiness Report 2026 and Jonathan Haidt's research
- Link: https://jonathanhaidt.substack.com/p/social-media-mental-illness-epidemic
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — CDC government survey data

### 6. Loneliness Unchanged Despite Surgeon General's Warning

- Three years after the Surgeon General declared loneliness a public health crisis (May 2023), **approximately 50% of American adults still report measurable loneliness** — unchanged
- No comprehensive federal legislation has been enacted to address structural causes
- Source: Altitudes Magazine reporting on follow-up data (2026)
- Link: https://www.altitudesmagazine.com/half-american-adults-still-report-loneliness-three-years-after-federal/
- **Verification:** Tier 2 — journalism citing government data

### 7. Social Media Harm Is Now Considered Causal, Not Just Correlational

- In January 2026, researchers (Haidt & Rausch) published evidence establishing **causation** between social media and adolescent mental health harm — not just correlation
- The 2026 World Happiness Report devoted its entire focus to social media's impact on happiness
- Problematic social media use is associated with lower wellbeing across all 43 countries studied, with negative effects intensifying between 2018 and 2022
- Source: World Happiness Report 2026; Jonathan Haidt & Zachary Rausch (2026)
- Link: https://worldhappiness.report/ed/2026/executive-summary-happiness-and-social-media/
- **Verification:** Tier 1 — peer-reviewed research report
