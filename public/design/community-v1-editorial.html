No. 001 Margaux Brooklyn, NY 7:42 a.m.

My upstairs neighbor has been crying through the wall for three nights. Tuesday I baked too many scones on purpose and left four on her mat with a post-it that said "made extra — no need to reply." She slid a thank-you under my door the next morning. We still haven't spoken. I think that might be the whole point.

I sat on the porch with my dad for forty minutes without talking about anything. He needed that more than advice.
— Anonymous · Austin, TX · 8:03 a.m.
No. 003 Rafa Oakland, CA 8:12 a.m.

My barista's kid has been sick. I left a card with cash folded inside. No note, no name. First time I've done something like that in my life and I'm 38. Kept thinking about it all week — how small the gesture was compared to how much it rattled me to make it.

No. 004 Eitan Chicago, IL 8:19 a.m.

Called my sister instead of texting. She picked up on the third ring sounding surprised. We talked about nothing for eleven minutes. I could hear her making soup in the background. That's it. That's the whole entry.

No. 005 Anonymous Glasgow, UK 8:33 a.m.

A woman at the bus stop was trying not to cry. I didn't say anything — I just stood a little closer than British politeness usually allows, and when the bus came I let her on first. She turned and nodded at me. That was the whole conversation.

No. 006 Priya Toronto, ON 9:01 a.m.

I stopped pretending I was busy when my roommate came home. Closed the laptop, asked about her day, and actually listened for the answer. She looked at me like I was a stranger for a second, then sat down and talked for an hour. I don't know how I became someone who makes her feel like an interruption.
